NOROT.

Mindful Phone Launcher

Status: In Development

Smartphones are designed to steal your attention. Norot is a retro-styled launcher that gives it back. It breaks the cycle of endless scrolling by slowing you down and removing the flashy traps.

PROJECTED SCORE
// Minimalist Base

A Calmer Home Screen

Say goodbye to flashy icons designed to grab your attention. Norot replaces your home screen with a simple, flowing list of text. It's peaceful, clean, and helps you use your phone with purpose.

  • ► Dynamic Backgrounds: Smooth, organic gradients create a calm, low-stimulation environment.
  • ► Wave App List: A vertically scrolling list that requires deliberate, intentional action to navigate.
// Mindful Pauses

The 'Take a Breath' Screen

Opening apps out of habit? Norot catches you. When you tap a distracting app, it asks you to wait before letting you in.

  • ► The Friction Delay: Forces a 10 to 30-second wait before entry. This short pause is usually enough to break the spell and make you realize you don't actually want to scroll.
  • ► Emergency Access: If you absolutely need the app, you can set a strict 15-minute allowance. Once the time is up, the app closes.
// Visual Neutralization

Boring Mode (Grayscale)

Apps use bright colors to keep you hooked. Norot can automatically turn your screen black and white. Without color, your phone suddenly becomes a lot less interesting.

  • ► Automated Triggers: Set your screen to turn gray at bedtime or after you've used your phone too much during the day.
  • ► Allowance Discouragement: Even if you bypass a block to open an app, your screen stays gray, making it easier to close it when you're done.
STIMULATION
NEUTRALIZED
// Stay on Track

Making It Hard to Quit

If you really need to break your focus session early, Norot makes you work for it. It's just annoying enough to keep you on track.

  • ► Cognitive Checkpoints: To exit early, you must solve a math problem or perfectly type out a long sentence without any mistakes.
  • ► The Patience Test: A 30-second "Hold Still" button. Let go for a millisecond, and the timer resets completely.
// App Configuration

Set Your Own Rules

Decide exactly how and when you want to use your apps. Group them together, set daily time limits, or create schedules (like 'Only allow Instagram from 6 PM to 8 PM'). Set up your boundaries once, and let Norot enforce them for you.

Time's Up

When your daily limit is reached or your schedule ends, the app is gently but firmly blocked. Time to get back to the real world.
